Top Cannabis Travel Destinations Worldwide

Cannabis Cambridge UK provides educational guidance on legal cannabis travel. Always verify local regulations before consuming or purchasing cannabis products.

Amsterdam, Netherlands

Travel Tip: Cannabis is tolerated in licensed coffee shops only. Public consumption is restricted in some districts. Never purchase from street dealers.

Barcelona, Spain

Travel Tip: Cannabis is permitted in private cannabis clubs. Tourists must be invited by a member; public consumption is illegal.

Berlin, Germany

Travel Tip: Germany has evolving cannabis regulations. Consumption rules vary; check current federal guidelines before travel.

Lisbon, Portugal

Travel Tip: Portugal decriminalized possession for personal use but does not allow open public sales. Respect possession limits.

Zurich, Switzerland

Travel Tip: Low-THC cannabis products are legal. High-THC cannabis remains regulated under strict pilot programs.

Denver, USA

Travel Tip: Cannabis is legal for adults 21+. Consumption is limited to private property or licensed lounges. Do not cross state lines with cannabis.

Los Angeles, USA

Travel Tip: Licensed dispensaries require ID. Public use is illegal. Delivery services are common but must be legally registered.

Toronto, Canada

Travel Tip: Cannabis is federally legal in Canada. Purchase only from licensed stores. Airports prohibit carrying cannabis internationally.

Vancouver, Canada

Travel Tip: Legal retail shops are widely available. Consumption rules vary by municipality—avoid parks and public spaces.

Bangkok, Thailand

Travel Tip: Cannabis laws have shifted recently. Recreational use may be restricted again; verify current policy before travel.

Phuket, Thailand

Travel Tip: Licensed dispensaries operate, but public smoking can lead to fines. Always consume privately.

Kathmandu, Nepal

Travel Tip: Cannabis remains illegal despite historic associations. Avoid possession to prevent legal consequences.

Tel Aviv, Israel

Travel Tip: Israel has a strong medical cannabis program. Recreational use remains restricted under regulatory frameworks.
